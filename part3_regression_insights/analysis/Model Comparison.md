

## Model Comparison
## Objective
The objective of the regression analysis is to identify factors most strongly associated with monthly
sales performance across retail stores.
## Model 1: Simple Regression – Marketing Spend
## Dependent Variable
## Monthly Sales
## Independent Variable
## Marketing Spend
## Interpretation
This model evaluates whether higher marketing investment is associated with higher monthly sales.
## Business Usefulness
Useful for estimating the relationship between promotional investment and revenue generation.
## Limitations
Does not account for footfall, inventory availability, customer ratings, or store characteristics.
## Model 2: Simple Regression – Footfall
## Dependent Variable
## Monthly Sales
## Independent Variable
## Footfall
## Interpretation
This model evaluates whether stores with higher customer traffic tend to achieve higher sales.
## Business Usefulness
Useful for understanding the relationship between customer visits and sales outcomes.

## Model 3: Multiple Regression
## Dependent Variable
## Monthly Sales
## Independent Variables
## Marketing Spend
## Footfall
## Inventory Availability Percentage
## Average Discount Percentage
## Customer Rating
## Region Dummy Variables
## Store Type Dummy Variables
## Interpretation
This model evaluates the combined impact of operational, marketing, and store-level factors on sales
performance.
## Business Usefulness
Provides a more realistic business model because multiple drivers influence sales simultaneously.
## Limitations
Regression identifies association and not direct causation.

## Final Model Selected
## Multiple Regression Model
## Reason:
The multiple regression model captures several business drivers simultaneously and generally provides
stronger explanatory power than individual simple regression models.
This model is therefore selected as the final business decision-support model.
